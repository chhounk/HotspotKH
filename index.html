About This Project

The Cambodia Hotspot Monitor displays near-real-time thermal hotspot detections across Cambodia using satellite data from NASA's FIRMS (Fire Information for Resource Management System).

What are hotspots? NASA satellites detect thermal anomalies — any heat source visible from space. Most detections in Cambodia are agricultural burns (farmers clearing fields), not wildfires.

Hotspot classification:

  • Likely Wildfire — High confidence + FRP ≥ 15 MW
  • Possible Wildfire — High confidence, or nominal + FRP ≥ 10 MW
  • Thermal Anomaly — Low confidence/intensity (likely agricultural)

Province boundaries from GADM. Protected areas from WDPA.

Hotspot locations are approximate satellite detections and may include false positives. Always verify with local authorities before taking action.
