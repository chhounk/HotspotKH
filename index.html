About This Project

The Cambodia Hotspot Monitor displays near-real-time thermal hotspot detections across Cambodia using satellite data from NASA's FIRMS (Fire Information for Resource Management System).

What are hotspots? NASA satellites detect thermal anomalies — any heat source visible from space. Most detections in Cambodia are agricultural burns (farmers clearing fields after harvest), not wildfires. Industrial activity (brick kilns, factories) and ritual or ceremonial burning (land clearing for ceremonies, religious practices) may also appear as thermal anomalies.

Hotspot classification & map legend:

Likely Wildfire — High satellite confidence + fire radiative power ≥ 15 MW
Possible Wildfire — High confidence, or nominal confidence + FRP ≥ 10 MW
Thermal Anomaly — Low confidence or intensity; likely agricultural, industrial, or ritual burning
Dot size reflects FRP intensity — larger dots indicate stronger heat sources.

Confidence levels:

Confidence is assigned by the NASA satellite algorithm and indicates how certain the detection is a real fire:
h  High — Strong thermal signal, very likely a real fire
n  Nominal — Moderate signal, probably a fire but less certain
l  Low — Weak signal, may be a false positive (sun glint, hot surfaces, cloud edges)
MODIS satellite detections use a numeric scale (0–100) instead of letters. Values above 80 are treated as High, above 30 as Nominal, and below as Low.
